  My love for computer programming began at a young age when a family neighbor gave me my first programming book, “Coding for Dummies for Kids.” Before then, programming had never been something I was interested in. As I began to work through the concepts and projects in the book, I realized that code wasn’t an impossible language of numbers, letters, and symbols, but rather something that even I could create. With enough persistence and knowledge, I could build anything I could imagine. The journey to pursue this interest has not been easy. I have had to make many sacrifices to continue learning. I had to rent Chromebooks and books from the public library when I didn't have access to a computer or material to learn from. Soon, I needed more, and began purchasing books about programming. Eventually, I was led to learn from Khan Academy and Codecademy based on the book's references. All of this preparation aided me when I began computer programming classes at my local community college during my first 3 years in high school. I have earned a Certificate of Achievement in Programming Foundations from my community college during high school. Now, I am advancing my knowledge in computer programming in college. Now with all my programming experience, I have begun to learn how to build AI.
